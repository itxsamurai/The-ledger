The Truth
About Exotic Pets

Owning an exotic animal is a lifelong commitment. Some species can be kept responsibly, while many others suffer in captivity or fuel the illegal wildlife trade. Understanding the difference is essential.

Reality

What Most People Don't Realize

Many exotic animals are bought because they look unique, but their care is far more demanding than most owners expect.

Specialised Care

Temperature, humidity, diet, lighting and enclosure size all matter.

Long Lifespans

Some parrots, tortoises and reptiles can live for decades.

Veterinary Care

Finding qualified exotic veterinarians can be difficult and expensive.
Balanced View

When Can Exotic Pet Ownership Be Ethical?

Usually Acceptable

  • Captive-bred animals
  • Legal ownership
  • Responsible breeders
  • Proper housing
  • Experienced owners
  • Lifetime commitment

Should Be Avoided

  • Wild-caught animals
  • Illegal wildlife trade
  • Endangered species
  • Impulse purchases
  • Poor welfare
  • Owners without proper knowledge

Our Position

Speaking for Those Without a Voice

Students for Sabah Wildlife believes wildlife belongs in the wild whenever possible. We recognise that some captive-bred species can be cared for responsibly by knowledgeable owners. However, we firmly oppose the illegal wildlife trade, the capture of wild animals for the pet trade, and the keeping of species whose physical and behavioural needs cannot realistically be met in captivity. Our mission is to educate, advocate and inspire people to make informed and ethical choices that protect Sabah's unique wildlife.
