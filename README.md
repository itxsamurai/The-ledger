Exotic Pet Trade Awareness Project
The problem: The exotic pet trade causes harm at almost every stage — animals taken from the wild, poor survival rates in transport, owners unprepared for what the animal actually needs, and impacts on wild populations and ecosystems. A lot of this isn't driven by bad intent — it's driven by people simply not knowing the legal status, care requirements, or permitting process before they acquire an animal.
Our approach: Spread awareness by making the information people need easy to find in one place, instead of scattered across government sites, forums, and breeder marketing. Specifically:

What's actually legal — species status by region, since laws vary by country and often by state/province
What real care requires — enclosure, diet, vet access, lifespan — stated honestly, not softened to make a sale
How to get permitted properly — the actual steps, so people don't skip straight to acquiring the animal

Why this matters: Informed owners make better decisions before they commit — whether that's choosing a species that's actually a good fit, going through proper legal channels, or deciding not to get the animal at all. Fewer uninformed acquisitions means fewer abandoned or surrendered exotic animals, and less pressure on wild populations from the illegal trade.# The-ledger
